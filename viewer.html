One Sentence Answers

What is the primary function of stomata?

Answer: Regulate transpiration and gas exchange.

Explanation: Stomata are small openings controlled by guard cells that help in the exchange of gases and control the loss of water vapor.
Key Point: Guard cells regulate the opening and closing of stomata.

Brief Answers

Describe guard cells and their function.

  • Bean-shaped cells enclosing the stomatal pore.
  • Change shape to open or close the stomata.
  • Play a key role in regulating gas exchange and transpiration.
Explanation: Guard cells swell or shrink due to changes in water pressure (turgor), thus controlling the stomatal aperture.
Key Point: Guard cells help plants maintain water balance and adapt to environmental conditions.

Detailed Answers

Explain components and functions of the epidermal system.

  • Epidermal Cells: Outer layer cells that protect internal tissues from mechanical injury and desiccation.
  • Trichomes: Hair-like projections that reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores and excess sunlight.
  • Stomata: Microscopic pores that facilitate gas exchange and control water vapor loss.
  • Cuticle: A waxy layer that minimizes water loss and acts as a barrier to pathogens.
Explanation: The epidermal system acts as the first line of defense and also aids in key physiological processes like transpiration and photosynthesis regulation.
Key Points:
  • Helps in protection, water regulation, and environmental interaction.
